What are Instagram Stories & Why They Work So Well

Instagram Stories was announced in the summer of 2016 but it became a hit over the course of 2017. The feature is similar to Snapchat, another popular app available in today’s context and focuses on ephemeral visual content. This means that users are able to create stories based on a series of images and videos, share them with friends and followers over a specific period of time. After the exact period of 24 hours, these stories are deleted. By June 2017, Instagram Stories had over 250 million daily active users, more than half of the total number of users of this entire social media channel. These numbers give us good reasons to pay attention to Stories and consider using the feature in our marketing campaigns. 15 million businesses are already registered with Instagram through business profiles out of which, 1 million per month use the advertising features. However, I know many will ask: “Why choosing to post ephemeral content and not regular posts?”; “Why invest in ads that will disappear in 24 hours never to be seen again?” Well, as strange as it may sound to some of you out there, ephemeral content may prove to be as effective as the regular posts. In some cases, depending on the nature of your eCommerce business, they may prove to be even better. The answer to your questions is “authenticity.” There was a study performed at Cornell University where a series of students were interviewed and asked why they like Snapchat. They said that the ephemeral content was fun but at the same time, it made them feel like the interaction was less formal. Snapchat is in many ways similar to Instagram Stories and this is why the study also applies here. There is an authenticity attached to the ephemeral status of these posts that comes directly from their less formal state. Brands are sometimes blamed for trying too much. They are blamed for a lack of authenticity in their marketing and advertising campaigns. This issue may lead to a loss of trust among customers and fans and ultimately, a drop in sales. Nobody expects you to spend days or weeks designing the slides of a story that will be automatically deleted after 24 hours. Therefore, as a marketer, you get to transmit a more authentic message due to the spontaneity of the Stories. You get to test ideas you are not absolutely sure about, test your creativity and most of all, act on quick ideas and sometimes on instinct.

Design your Ads

Now that we know why Instagram ads and Instagram Stories Ads may be effective for your eCommerce business, it’s time to take a step further and consider designing your ads. First, you need to know that there are two types of ads you can post inside Instagram Stories: videos and images. While videos can be no longer than 15 seconds, a static ad will be displayed for only 5 seconds. It’s not a long time and this is why you need to design it so that it will be able to grab the viewer’s attention immediately. Whether you choose video or photo, the accepted format is a standard 9:16 full screen vertical with a recommended resolution of 1080 x 1920 pixels. The minimum accepted resolution is 600 x 1067 pixels. Also, keep in mind that the recommended file types are .mp4 and .mov for video ads, .jpg and .png for photo ads. The maximum file sizes should not exceed 30 MB for photos and 4 GB for videos.

How to use Instagram Stories Ads and why

Like any other marketing or advertising campaign, the one you will undergo through Instagram Stories can be setup to suit multiple goals. Let’s take a look at some of these options and why they may be beneficial for your business. Also, we are going to take a quick look at some of the Instagram Stories Ads success stories, to better understand how these benefits look in real life scenarios.

Why invest in brand awareness?

More than half of marketers admit they are planning to invest more in brand awareness. They are doing it because a strong brand attracts more customers. At the same time, you need your audience to know about you, know what you have to offer and who you are. They need to trust you in order to become regulars - but before trust, comes awareness. Trust is then built through intense commitment to quality of service and constant communication with your customers. In short, you will increase customer loyalty, your visibility and your impact on the targeted audience. However, the ultimate goal is to increase your market lead. The more people know about you, the higher the market lead is. And, when a market lead is high, the sales potential increases. Offer the people a positive experience when they make a purchase and they will continue to spread awareness of your brand. Here's a real life example: A great example in this context comes from YOOX, an online store for fashion, design and art. They wanted to position themselves as a top destination for stylish shoppers. To this end, they started a new Instagram Stories Ads campaign that proved to be quite successful. How did they do it and what were the results? For their Stories Ads campaign, YOOX decided to adapt a TV spot into a video ad that was targeted to men and women ages 25 to 44 years old. Also, they selected their demographics based on interests, which in case were fashion and shopping. The result was a significant 6-point growth in brand awareness and an 11-point increase in ad recall.

Increase in sales

Let’s face it: this is the first thing you think about, as a marketer for an eCommerce business. Here's another real life example: Maybe you’ve already heard about Virgin Holidays, the leading transatlantic tour operator from the UK. The goal of their Instagram Stories Ads campaign was to drive conversions in-store and online. They wanted to increase sales but they also set a goal on growing brand awareness, two things that go hand in hand. Their campaign ran for only 3 days in March 2017. Through their video ads, they managed to successfully bring to life some popular landscapes from New York City, San Francisco and Las Vegas, 3 of the top destinations from their travel offer. The video ad introduced the viewer to the cities through powerful and intense depictions of places and people that were supposed to make us feel like we are already there. Ultimately, the campaign resulted in a “2.84X increase in return on ad spend, and the cost per view also fell by 10%.”

Why invest in promoting an app?

A mobile app will allow you to push notifications and thus, keep your audience connected with your brand and store. It will also allow you to make your store more mobile friendly and update it for mobile use for maximum readability and functionality. Considering that 90% of the time spent on mobile by the average user is on apps, the idea of building your own dedicated app may not seem bad at all. Real life example: This real-life example comes from Hopper, a travel company that built a mobile app that was launched in 2015. Their campaign on Instagram Stories Ads targeted “millennials,” a group known for making purchases through mobile apps. They promoted hundreds of deals every week through illustration depicting dreamy destinations and locations from all around the world. While other travel companies focus on people who are ready to make purchases immediately, Hopper targets a young audience that likes to plan ahead. Their app keeps them engaged by sending price alerts when the prices drop through push notifications. The results were better than expected. They reached more than 7.5 million people in North America and the Stories Ads generated a 65% increase in app installs and over 80% increase in bookings for new users.

Generate Leads

What does “leads generation” means? Well, this is a term usually used in marketing that refers mainly to the initiation of the consumer’s interest or inquiry in your products or services. It’s a very important term and consequently, a very important process in attracting more customers or gathering important demographic information that may lead eventually to new customers. “Lead generation is the process of attracting and converting strangers and prospects into someone who has indicated interest is your company's product or service.” (Hubspot)

Why invest in lead generation?

Most of the people who want to make a purchase, are halfway there when they contact a salesperson. A lead generation campaign, grabs the audience’s attention long before they know they are ready to buy your products. Thus, it can accelerate sales and consequently, increase profits by decreasing the time between product awareness and the decision to acquire it. Real life example: This example comes from Mercedes-Benz. The campaign they underwent on Instagram Stories Ads was meant to increase lead generation for their new C-Class Cabriolet model. To this end, they focused their ads on the enjoyment of open-top driving. While the campaign was intended at first as an experiment, the story offered its audience a seamless experience, while showcasing the car in an exciting and highly aspirational way”. What were the results? Well, Mercedes-Benz managed to achieve a 9-point increase in ad recall, a 3-point increase in message association with their exclusive tagline and a reach of more n=than 2.6 million Instagram users.
